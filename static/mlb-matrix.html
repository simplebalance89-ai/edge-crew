NRFI Matrix — The Primary Play

No Runs First Inning | 15 Variables | First-Inning-Specific Data
NRFI is our primary MLB bet type. This matrix is completely different from the full-game matrix — it only cares about what happens in the first inning. SP first-inning stats, top-of-order matchups, and park/umpire/weather for that one frame.
TIER 1 — SP FIRST-INNING DOMINANCE (Weight 9-10)
10
SP First-Inning ERA
ERA specifically in the first inning over last 2-3 seasons. Some pitchers: 4.50 overall ERA but 1.80 first-inning ERA. THE number for NRFI.
Source: FanGraphs (inning splits)
9
SP First-Inning K%
Strikeout rate per batter faced in the first inning. High K = quick outs = no baserunners.
Source: FanGraphs (inning splits)
9
SP First-Inning BB%
Walk rate in the first inning. Walks = free baserunners = NRFI killer. Lower is better.
Source: FanGraphs (inning splits)
9
NRFI Historical Rate (Matchup)
Historical NRFI % for this specific SP pair. Some matchups hit 75%+ NRFI rate. Gold mine when data exists.
Source: Historical game data
TIER 2 — TOP-OF-ORDER MATCHUP (Weight 7-8)
8
Top-of-Order wOBA vs SP Hand
First 3 batters' wOBA against this pitcher's handedness. Lefty-heavy top 3 vs weak LHB pitcher = YRFI danger.
Source: FanGraphs splits
8
SP First-Inning HR/9
Home run rate in the first inning specifically. One solo HR kills NRFI. Lower = better.
Source: FanGraphs (inning splits)
7
SP First-Pitch Strike %
Getting ahead early in counts = quick outs. High FPS% pitchers cruise through first innings.
Source: Statcast
7
SP First-Inning GB Rate
Ground balls = double play potential, no HRs. High GB rate in first inning suppresses scoring.
Source: FanGraphs
7
Park Factor (1st Inning)
Some parks play differently in the first inning than overall. Coors 1st inning NRFI rate isn't as bad as you'd think.
Source: Historical park data
TIER 3 — CONTEXT (Weight 5-6)
6
Umpire 1st Inning Strike Zone
Wide zone = more K's, fewer walks. Tight zone = more traffic. Check umpire's first-inning tendencies specifically.
Source: UmpireScorecards
6
Weather (Wind + Temp)
Wind blowing out + hot day = YRFI territory. Wind in + cold = NRFI boost. Only outdoor parks.
Source: Weather API
6
Leadoff Hitter OBP
If the leadoff man gets on, the whole inning changes. High OBP leadoff = NRFI risk.
Source: FanGraphs
6
SP First-Batter Retirement Rate
Does this pitcher retire batter #1? If not, the inning gets messy fast.
Source: Historical game data
5
Lineup Top-3 ISO (Power)
Power in the top of the order can end NRFI with one swing. High ISO = HR threat.
Source: FanGraphs
5
SP Pitch Count Efficiency (1st Inning)
Pitchers who throw 20+ pitches in the first inning are in trouble. Efficient = 10-12 pitches, scoreless.
Source: Statcast
