Two Engines, One Grade

AI ENGINE Sinton.ia's Analysis

Full AI analysis powered by Grok reasoning + DeepSeek formatting. Reads every game through a 15-variable scoring matrix with access to live injury data, line movement, and rest schedules from ESPN, The Odds API, and CBS Sports. Each variable scored 1-10, weighted by importance, rolled into a composite score.

Sinton.ia doesn't just grade teams — he identifies the specific question: "Where is the market wrong on this game?" Every game gets an edge question, not just a letter.

MATH ENGINE Alyssa's Edge Calculator

Pure expected value math. No opinions, no gut feel. Three layers:

  • Multi-Book Comparison — pulls lines from 12+ books (DraftKings, FanDuel, Pinnacle, Bovada, Bet365, Hard Rock, BetOnline, William Hill...). Compares implied probabilities. Where books disagree = opportunity.
  • Mathurin Screener — for every player prop: pulls last 5 game logs from ESPN, computes the FLOOR (lowest of L5), compares to the book's line. Floor > line = MISPRICED.
  • Injury Gap Detection — when a star is OUT, identifies which role players absorb minutes. If the book hasn't adjusted their props = free money.

The 15-Variable Matrix

NBA-specific factors, each scored 1-10:

Star Player Status
Who's playing, who's out, who's limited. This moves lines more than anything.
Rest Advantage
Days off, B2B, 3-in-4. Fatigue is the NBA's biggest hidden variable.
Injury Impact
Not just who's out — how many PPG/RPG/APG are missing from the lineup?
Line Movement
Opening line vs current. Significant moves = sharp money or injury news.
Sharp vs Public
Where sharp bettors are vs the public. Fading public heavy sides.
Offensive Efficiency
Points per 100 possessions. The real measure of offense.
Defensive Efficiency
Points allowed per 100 possessions.
Recent Form
L10, L5 performance. Hot/cold streaks matter in the NBA.
Tempo Matchup
Pace differential. Fast vs slow creates variance.
Home Court
Home court advantage — still real, but varies by team.
ATS Trend
Against-the-spread performance. Books adjust slowly.
Bench Depth
Rotation depth. Short rotations + B2B = blowout risk.
Rebounding
Board differential. Second chances at the rim.
Turnover Margin
Possession control. Turnovers = fast break points.
Three Pt Reliance
3PT volume and accuracy. High variance = upset risk.

Additional factors: Travel Distance, B2B Fatigue, Coaching Matchup, FT Shooting, Rivalry/Motivation, Line vs Model

NBA-Specific Edge Signals

  • Injury absorption — star OUT, role players feast. Books adjust the star's line fast but are SLOW on the role players. Example: Sabonis OUT → Achiuwa O6.5 REB cashed at +106.
  • B2B fatigue — second night of a back-to-back, especially on the road. Unders and dog spreads.
  • "Too Good to Be True" — when the spread looks like free money, the book is giving it away for a reason. Denver -3 looked free → Denver down 14. Trap.
  • Prop floor test — Mathurin L5: 26, 28, 21, 22, 23. Line: 17.5. Floor (21) > line (17.5) by 3.5 points. The book is behind. CASHED.
  • Multi-book ML gaps — 15+ cent gap between books on the same moneyline = someone is wrong. Bet the better number.

The Rule

The Mathurin Test: Before placing any bet — "Can you show a NUMBER that proves the book is wrong?"

YES: "His L5 floor is 21, book has 17.5. Gap is +3.5." → BET IT.
NO: "I think Denver covers." → PASS. That's an opinion, not an edge.
