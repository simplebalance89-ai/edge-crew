NCAAB Grading System

How we grade college basketball — two engines, one grade

Two Engines, One Grade

Every NCAAB game is analyzed by two independent engines that approach the game differently:

AI ENGINE Sinton.ia's Analysis

AI-powered analysis using a 15-variable scoring matrix. Each variable is scored 1-10, then weighted into a composite score. The AI reads injury reports, rest schedules, travel, matchup data, and line movement to identify where the market might be wrong.

MATH ENGINE Alyssa's Edge Calculator

Pure math — no opinions. Compares the book's implied probability to our calculated probability based on:

  • Multi-book line comparison (12+ sportsbooks)
  • Consensus vs best-available odds
  • Historical ATS trends
  • Player prop screening (L5 floor vs book line)

The two engines agree? Strong signal. They disagree? Caution flag.

The 15-Variable Matrix

Each game is scored across these 15 factors (1-10 scale):

Tempo Matchup
Pace differential — fast vs slow creates edges
Three Pt Reliance
3PT dependency — high variance = upset risk
Sharp vs Public
Where sharp money is vs public — fade the public
Line Movement
Opening vs current line — movement = information
ATS Trend
Against-the-spread performance (L10, L5)
Defensive Efficiency
Points allowed per 100 possessions
Offensive Efficiency
Points scored per 100 possessions
Star Player Status
Key player availability and health
Bench Depth
Rotation depth — thin bench = fatigue risk
Conference Strength
Strength of conference — Big Ten vs mid-major
Turnover Margin
Turnover differential — possession matters
Rebounding
Board control — second chances = points
Recent Form
L5 game performance trend
Home Court
Home court advantage factor
Coaching Matchup
Coach track record in this matchup type

Additional factors scored but weighted lower: FT Shooting, Rivalry/Motivation, B2B Fatigue, Travel Distance, Line vs Model

Grade Scale

A
Strong edge — book is wrong. Composite 8+. Both engines agree. Clear mispricing identified. Bet this.
A-
Good edge. Composite 7.5+. Strong signal but one variable is uncertain (e.g., injury status TBD).
B+
Lean. Composite 7+. Slight edge detected. Worth a small play if the price is right.
B
Marginal. Composite 6+. Some factors favor a side but not enough for a confident play.
C
No edge. Composite 5-6. Fair line. The book got it right. Pass.
D/F
Trap. Composite below 5. This looks good on the surface but the data says stay away.
The Mathurin Test: Before any bet, ask: "Can I show a NUMBER that proves the book is wrong?" If the answer is just "I think they'll win" — that's a grade, not an edge. We only bet edges.

NCAAB-Specific Edges

  • Conference tournament fatigue — teams playing 3rd game in 3 days, legs are gone
  • Venue neutrality — "home" team in a neutral site tournament doesn't get full home court advantage
  • Tight rims — some arenas have notoriously tight rims, favoring unders
  • Public bias on blue bloods — Duke, Kentucky, UNC get bet heavily regardless of matchup. Fade when the line is inflated.
  • Mid-major efficiency — small conference teams can be drastically undervalued in early rounds
  • First half lines — 1H/1Q lines are where books are least efficient in college basketball
